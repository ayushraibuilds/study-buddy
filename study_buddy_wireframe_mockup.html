Summary
What does the First Law of Thermodynamics state?
Laws
Energy can neither be created nor destroyed, only converted from one form to another. Mathematically: dU = dQ - dW, where dU is change in internal energy, dQ is heat supplied, dW is work done by the system.
Define entropy and state its relationship to spontaneous processes.
Entropy
Entropy (S) is a measure of disorder or randomness in a system. For spontaneous processes, the total entropy of the universe always increases (dS_universe > 0). This is the Second Law of Thermodynamics.
For an ideal gas undergoing adiabatic expansion, what happens to temperature?
Adiabatic
Temperature decreases. Since dQ = 0 (no heat exchange), from the first law: dU = -dW. The gas does work at the expense of internal energy, so temperature falls. Relation: TV^(γ-1) = constant.

In a Carnot engine operating between temperatures T1 (hot) and T2 (cold), the efficiency is:
A. T1 / T2
B. 1 - (T2 / T1)
C. (T1 - T2) / T2
D. T2 / (T1 + T2)
Carnot efficiency = 1 - (T_cold / T_hot). Option B is correct. Note that T must be in Kelvin, not Celsius — a common JEE mistake.

Chapter summary
Thermodynamics studies energy transfer in physical and chemical systems. The four laws govern how energy behaves: zeroth (thermal equilibrium), first (energy conservation), second (entropy always increases), and third (absolute zero is unattainable).

JEE focuses heavily on Carnot cycles, entropy calculations, and thermodynamic processes (isothermal, adiabatic, isobaric, isochoric). Expect 2–3 questions on Carnot efficiency and 1–2 on entropy change calculations.
